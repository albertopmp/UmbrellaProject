A brief explanation of Umbre??a
Umbre??a Purpose

The purpose of this project is to get hands-on experience with AWS Services. As I was learning to become AWS Cloud Practitioner Certified, I wanted to get my hands dirty and apply all the things I'd been learning during the preparation of the certificate.

I saw that many people have taken the Cloud Resume Challenge as a way to advance their cloud career. Nevertheless, I wanted to do something different and develop a project of my own, solving along the way an issue that I'd been facing since I was just a little boy: always forgetting my umbrella when it rains!

Moreover, I want this project to become a tool for consolidating the knowledge that I'll acquire during future training and certifications through hands-on practice.

How It Works

Umbre??a shows you whether you'll need to take your umbrella or not, just as simple as that! Additionally, you can subscribe with your email to recevie daily reminders so you don't forget your umbrella at home

In this first iteration of the development I kept the functionality simple because I wanted to focus on what really mattered here, the use of AWS Services. Right now Umbre??a only works for my city, Santiago de Compostela, and daily reminders are sent at 6:30 AM (Madrid)

Architecture

In order to design the architecture I used the knowledge acquired throughout the certification process and I took the time to thoroughly read the documentation of many services. The latter was really helpful both for the design and the exam, as I learned the intricacies of some services and how they worked at low-level.

I also want to highlight that I've only made use of AWS Free Tier, as I wanted this project to be accessible without incurring any costs (Except for the costs of the domain name - Route 53)
